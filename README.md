# 💫 About Me:
Bachelor of Science in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Lafayette College (GPA: 4.00, Dean’s List) and Oechsle Scholar. I am a multidisciplinary engineer with interests in hardware systems, embedded design, and the integration of software and control systems.<br> <br>My experience includes research in robotic motion control, where I worked on MATLAB-based algorithms for accurate positioning and speed tracking, as well as motor initialization and multi-joint sequencing to improve system calibration and reliability. Currently working on the kinematics and bulding a gripper as Bergh Family Fellow.<br> <br>I also enjoy low-level, hands-on engineering and am currently building a collision-avoidance system for a line-following robot using only analog and digital components, including 555 timers, comparators, and differential amplifiers, to replicate microcontroller functionality without embedded processors.<br><br>Alongside technical work, I serve in student leadership roles, including First-Year Representative for the Muslim Student Association and future Resident Advisor, and have experience in mentoring and organizing community initiatives.<br> <br>I am interested in robotics, embedded systems, and control engineering, and I am always seeking opportunities to build, learn, and collaborate.<br>
